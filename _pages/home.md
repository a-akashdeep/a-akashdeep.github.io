## What I work on

My research focuses on magnetic thin-film systems and spintronic materials, especially where magnetic order, interfaces, surfaces, and crystal symmetry influence transport and dynamics.

Selected areas:

- Spintronics and magnetic thin-film heterostructures
- Altermagnetism, RuO₂-based systems, and compensated magnetic materials
- Magnetotransport, spin currents, and Hall-related effects
- Ferrimagnetic garnets, spin dynamics, and magnonics
- Thin-film growth, vacuum systems, cryogenics, and lab instrumentation
- Advanced characterization using transport, spectroscopy, microscopy, and facility-based methods

## Selected work

### Spin transport in RuO₂/permalloy heterostructures  
Studied interface-generated spin-current magnetoresistance in magnetic thin-film heterostructures, connecting transport measurements with questions about altermagnetic materials.

### Surface-localized magnetic order in RuO₂  
Used low-energy muon probes to investigate depth-dependent magnetic order in RuO₂ thin films, combining advanced characterization with collaborative facility-based research.

### Ferrimagnetic garnet spin dynamics  
Worked on temperature-dependent spin dynamics in YIG/GdIG/Pt trilayers using ferromagnetic resonance, spin pumping, and spin-current-related measurements.

### Experimental infrastructure and workflows  
Contributed to thin-film growth and characterization infrastructure, including sputtering, RHEED/PLD-related workflows, cryogenic transport, lithography, and measurement setup development.
