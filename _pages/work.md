This page presents my work as a research and project portfolio. It is meant to show not only what I studied, but how I approach technical problems: through experiments, measurement design, data interpretation, collaboration, and communication.

## Research and technical themes

### Magnetic materials and spintronics

I work on magnetic thin-film systems where spin, magnetic order, interfaces, and crystal symmetry influence transport and dynamics. My research includes RuO₂-based altermagnetic systems, ferrimagnetic garnets, spin currents, magnetotransport, and advanced magnetic characterization.

### Experimental design and measurement workflows

Much of my work involves designing or improving measurement workflows, preparing samples, troubleshooting instruments, and interpreting field-, angle-, and temperature-dependent data.

### Instrumentation and lab systems

I have contributed to experimental infrastructure involving thin-film growth, vacuum systems, cryogenic transport, RHEED/PLD-related workflows, lithography, wire bonding, and characterization tools.

### Collaboration and mentoring

My work also includes supporting student projects, hosting visiting researchers, coordinating subgroup discussions, organizing journal club workflows, and contributing to collaborative publications.

## Selected projects

### RuO₂/permalloy spin-current magnetoresistance

**Summary:**  
Investigated angle-dependent magnetoresistance and interface-generated spin-current effects in RuO₂/permalloy heterostructures.

**What this project shows:**  
Experimental spintronics, magnetotransport, data interpretation, and the ability to separate competing physical mechanisms.

**Methods:**  
Magnetotransport, angle-dependent measurements, field-dependent measurements, temperature-dependent analysis, magnetic thin-film heterostructures.

**Related output:**  
First-author publication in *Physical Review Applied*.

---

### Surface-localized magnetic order in RuO₂ thin films

**Summary:**  
Studied surface-localized magnetic order in RuO₂ thin films using low-energy muon probes.

**What this project shows:**  
Advanced characterization, facility-based research, collaboration, and interpretation of depth-sensitive magnetic measurements.

**Methods:**  
Low-energy muon spectroscopy, thin-film systems, temperature-dependent analysis, collaborative experimental work.

**Related output:**  
First-author manuscript / publication on RuO₂ thin films and low-energy muon probes.

---

### Ferrimagnetic garnet spin dynamics

**Summary:**  
Studied temperature-dependent spin dynamics and spin-current generation in YIG/GdIG/Pt trilayers.

**What this project shows:**  
Experience with ferrimagnetic materials, spin dynamics, FMR-based analysis, and spin-current-related measurement techniques.

**Methods:**  
Ferromagnetic resonance, spin pumping, spin Seebeck effect, inverse spin Hall detection, temperature-dependent measurements.
